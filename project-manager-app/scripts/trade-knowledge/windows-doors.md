## OVERVIEW

Window and door replacement is one of the highest-dispute-rate trades in residential construction. Water intrusion after installation is the #1 callback. Every step in this guide is oriented toward preventing that outcome.

**Correct sequence:**
1. Measure existing rough opening → 2. Order with correct rough opening dimensions → 3. Remove existing unit → 4. Flash pan (sill) → 5. Install WRB (weather-resistive barrier) integration → 6. Set unit → 7. Shim and level → 8. Flash head and jambs → 9. Flash head with drip cap → 10. Insulate → 11. Interior trim → 12. Exterior caulk and paint prep

---

## MEASURING ROUGH OPENINGS

**Measure three times — order once.**

### Existing Window Replacement
- Measure inside the frame (not the old window): width at top, middle, and bottom; height at left, center, and right.
- Use the smallest dimension — that's your constraint.
- Order window at smallest measured dimension minus 1/2" (allows 1/4" shimming on each side).
- Check for out-of-square: measure diagonals. Difference > 1/2" means the opening needs adjustment before unit installation.

### New Construction or Reframe
- Frame rough opening = unit size + 1/2" width + 1/2" height (for shimming).
- Add 1/4" extra if using foam insulation (slightly more forgiving on final shimming).
- King studs: full-height on each side.
- Jack studs (trimmers): cut to header height.
- Header: sized per span and load. For 4' openings in load-bearing walls: double 2×10 minimum or per engineer.
- Sill: 2×4 or 2×6 flat-laid, supported by cripple studs.

### Door Rough Openings
- Standard prehung door: add 2" to door width and 2-1/2" to door height for rough opening.
- Example: 2-8 (32") door → 34" wide RO; 6-8 (80") height → 82.5" RO.
- ADA door: minimum 32" clear (typically a 36" door in a 38" RO).
- Exterior door: check sill height matches flooring finish — 1-3/4" thick door sill + transition + flooring + subfloor all need to fit under RO height.

## FLASHING — THE MOST CRITICAL STEP

Improper flashing is responsible for 80% of water intrusion callbacks on window and door installations. Follow this sequence precisely.

### Pan Flashing (Sill First)
1. Apply peel-and-stick flashing tape across the full width of the sill (inside the RO), with minimum 6" up each jamb.
2. Fold corners: do not cut; fold to create a waterproof dam at the corners.
3. This is the "bathtub" that catches any water that gets past the window unit.
4. Slope toward exterior if possible (or use sloped sill pan).

### WRB (Weather-Resistive Barrier) Integration — "I"-Method
1. WRB must be pre-cut in an inverted "I" shape at the window opening before unit is installed.
2. Fold upper flap up and temporarily tape to wall (will be lapped over head flashing later).
3. Fold side flaps into the RO and tape to jamb framing.
4. Bottom flap: lap over pan flashing or fold and tape — this piece is sacrificial.

### Install Unit
- Set window/door into opening on top of pan flashing.
- Shim, level, plumb, and square before fastening.

### Jamb Flashing (Sides)
- Apply peel-and-stick flashing tape over window flange and onto WRB on each jamb.
- Lap over pan flashing at bottom corners.
- Press firmly into all corners.

### Head Flashing
- Apply peel-and-stick tape across full head of unit, overlapping jamb flashing by 1" minimum.
- If window has a head fin (flange): cover fin with flashing tape.
- Release upper WRB flap and fold down over head flashing tape. Tape WRB flap down.

### Drip Cap (Exterior Head)
- Install metal drip cap over head flange and under WRB.
- Drip cap directs water away from the window head and over the siding below.
- Do NOT caulk under the drip cap — must allow water that infiltrates siding to drain out freely.

### Exterior Caulk
- Caulk jamb and head flanges to siding with paintable polyurethane sealant — NOT silicone (silicone can't be painted).
- Do NOT caulk below the bottom flange. Water must be able to drain out.
- Caulk at siding-to-trim joints around window, but leave a gap at the bottom for drainage.

## UNIT INSTALLATION — SHIMMING

### Windows
1. Set unit on sill pan flashing.
2. Check level at sill — this controls the whole window. Shim until perfectly level.
3. Check plumb on both jambs. Shim accordingly.
4. Check for square: diagonals should match within 1/8".
5. Fasten through flange at manufacturer's specified locations and spacing.
6. Open and close window — it should operate smoothly with no binding.
7. Insert shims at each screw location to prevent bowing of frame.
8. Shim gap: trim shims flush with frame using a utility knife.

### Prehung Exterior Doors
1. Set door in opening on sill flashing.
2. Level the sill (floor of threshold): critical — controls how door operates.
3. Plumb hinge side first (most critical): use 6-foot level.
4. Shim at hinge locations before fastening hinge side.
5. Fasten hinge side through casing or through jamb into jack stud.
6. Check latch/strike side for plumb and check reveal (gap between door and frame).
7. Shim at strike and top corners.
8. Swing door through full range of motion — should swing freely and latch without force.
9. Adjust if needed before any trim is applied.

## INSULATION AROUND FRAMES

- Fill gap between window/door frame and rough opening with low-expansion foam or fiberglass batt.
- **Use low-expansion foam only** — standard expanding foam can bow the frame and prevent the window from operating correctly. Use foam rated "for windows and doors."
- Fiberglass batt (stuffed in without compression) works well and has zero risk of frame distortion.
- Full coverage around all sides — gaps allow air infiltration and condensation inside the wall.
- Trim excess foam with a utility knife after curing. Cover with interior trim.

## EGRESS REQUIREMENTS

Any sleeping room (bedroom) must have at least one egress window (IRC R310):
- **Minimum net clear opening area**: 5.7 sq ft (5.0 sq ft for grade-floor openings).
- **Minimum net clear opening height**: 24".
- **Minimum net clear opening width**: 20".
- **Maximum sill height**: 44" above finished floor.
- **Egress window wells** (below grade): minimum 9 sq ft clear area; minimum 36" projection from foundation.

If replacing a window in a bedroom with a smaller unit, this is an egress code violation — **flag to client before ordering**.

## INTERIOR TRIM

1. **Install after all exterior flashing and caulking are complete.**
2. Measure and cut casing: cope or miter corners (miter is standard for most modern trim profiles).
3. Reveal: set casing back 3/16"–1/4" from inside edge of window frame (use a reveal guide).
4. Nail casing to window frame (1" nails) and to wall framing (2.5" nails into stud).
5. Apply painter's caulk at inside corner of casing-to-wall joint.
6. Fill nail holes with wood filler.
7. Paint before hardware installation.

### Stool and Apron (Traditional Style)
- Stool: horizontal piece running across sill inside. Must be notched around window frame.
- Apron: installed flat below stool, against wall.
- Stool length: extends past casing by 3/4"–1" on each side.

## INSPECTION CHECKLIST

### During Installation (QC Checkpoints)
- [ ] Pan flashing installed on sill, corners folded (not cut).
- [ ] WRB integrated in "I" pattern around opening.
- [ ] Unit level, plumb, and square before fastening.
- [ ] Shimmed at all fastener locations.
- [ ] Jamb and head flashing tape applied.
- [ ] Drip cap installed under WRB at head.
- [ ] Low-expansion foam fills all gaps.
- [ ] Window opens, closes, locks correctly.
- [ ] Door swings freely and latches without lifting or forcing.

### Final Inspection
- [ ] All flanges flashed and taped.
- [ ] WRB lapped correctly (head over jambs over sill — water flows down, never trapped).
- [ ] No caulk below bottom flange (allows drainage).
- [ ] Exterior caulk applied at head and jambs.
- [ ] Interior trim complete, caulked at wall.
- [ ] Insulation fills all gaps around frame.
- [ ] Hardware functional: locks, operators, screens.
- [ ] Egress windows meet size requirements (bedrooms).

## WATER INTRUSION — COMMON FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Cause | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Water enters at head | No drip cap or head flashing | Always install drip cap under WRB |
| Water enters at corner | Cut corners on pan flashing | Fold corners — never cut |
| Water enters at jamb | WRB lapped wrong (over instead of under flashing) | WRB must go UNDER jamb and head flashing |
| Water pools at sill inside | No pan flashing | Install pan flashing before unit |
| Frame rot within 3 years | Caulk applied at bottom flange | Never caulk bottom — allows drainage |
| Condensation on interior | Insulation gaps around frame | Fill all gaps with low-expansion foam |
| Unit bows, won't operate | Standard foam (over-expands) | Use low-expansion "window and door" foam |
