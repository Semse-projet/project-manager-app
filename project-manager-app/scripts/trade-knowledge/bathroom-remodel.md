## OVERVIEW — TRADE DEPENDENCIES

A bathroom remodel touches multiple trades in a specific sequence. Work out of order and you create rework, callbacks, and dispute risk.

**Correct sequence:**
1. Demolition → 2. Rough plumbing → 3. Rough electrical → 4. Framing/blocking → 5. Waterproofing → 6. Drywall/cement board → 7. Tile → 8. Fixtures/trim → 9. Final electrical → 10. Final inspection

---

## DEMOLITION SAFETY

1. **Shut off water**: Locate isolation valves for toilet (behind tank) and sink (under vanity). If none, use the main. Confirm no pressure by opening a faucet.
2. **Shut off electricity**: Turn off bathroom circuit(s) at panel. Verify with non-contact tester at every outlet, switch, and fixture.
3. **Protect adjacent areas**: Cover doorway with poly sheeting and tape; lay cardboard on hallway floor. Bathroom tile demo creates abundant sharp debris.
4. **Asbestos/lead check**: Floors with 9"×9" vinyl tiles or pre-1980 sheet vinyl → test before cutting. Texture on walls before 1980 → test before sanding.
5. **Floor protection**: Remove all fixtures before removing floor tile — not after. Porcelain tile shards will damage fixtures.
6. **Subfloor protection**: During demo, do not cut or damage the subfloor. Deteriorated subfloor is a common hidden condition — document immediately with photos.

## COMMON HIDDEN CONDITIONS IN BATHROOM DEMO

These discoveries typically trigger a change order. Photograph immediately, stop work, notify client before proceeding:

- **Subfloor rot**: wet area around toilet flange or tub surround. Requires replacement before any new work.
- **Black mold**: behind shower tile, in wall cavities, under vinyl floor. Remediation required before framing or drywall.
- **Improper previous plumbing**: no P-trap, no vent, incorrect slope, copper joined to galvanized without dielectric union.
- **No blocking in walls**: existing blocking for grab bars or fixtures missing — must add before closing walls.
- **Electrical violations**: no GFCI, wrong wire gauge, shared circuits, aluminum wiring.
- **Out-of-level floor**: more than 3/8" in 10 feet requires self-leveling compound before tile.

## PLUMBING ROUGH-IN

### Toilet
- Flange center: 12" from finished wall (15" ADA). If current flange is wrong distance, replace.
- Flange must sit at or slightly above finished floor level (not below — causes wax ring failure).
- After new tile: use flange extender ring if flange ends up below tile surface.
- Verify drain line slope: 1/4" per foot toward stack.
- Cleanout accessible — do not enclose without access panel.

### Shower/Tub
- Shower drain: 60" from wall (standard). Verify with new base/pan before framing.
- Tub drain: location varies by tub model — get exact tub dimensions before rough-in.
- Shower valve (thermostatic or pressure-balance): rough-in at 48" AFF to centerline (verify with specific valve manufacturer).
- Pressure-balance valve required at all showers (ASSE 1016) — anti-scald protection.
- Drain assembly: match drain size to new base/pan (2" most standard; check manufacturer).

### Vanity/Lavatory
- Drain rough-in: 17–20" from finished wall to drain centerline (verify with vanity model).
- Supply stubs: hot left, cold right at 21–22" AFF typically. Adjust for vessel sinks or wall-mount faucets.
- Shut-off valves: install 1/4-turn angle stops at both hot and cold supply stubs.

### Water Heater Check
- If remodel includes shower upgrade: verify water heater capacity (min 40 gal for standard bathroom; 50 gal if 2+ bathrooms).
- Anti-scald valve at water heater recommended — set at 120°F.

## ELECTRICAL ROUGH-IN

- **GFCI protection**: required at all receptacles within 6 feet of any sink (NEC 210.8). One GFCI outlet can protect downstream standard outlets.
- **Dedicated circuit**: recommended 20A for bathroom; required if sharing with other bathrooms (NEC 210.11(C)(3)).
- **Exhaust fan**: must vent OUTSIDE — not into attic (IRC M1507.2). Duct: insulated flex 4" minimum to exterior termination. CFM: minimum 50 CFM for most bathrooms; more for large or spa baths. Timer switch recommended.
- **Vanity lighting**: switched separately from exhaust fan is preferred. GFCI protection not required at light fixtures above tub/shower — but fixtures must be rated for wet/damp locations.
- **Recessed lighting over shower/tub**: must be rated IC-AT (insulated ceiling, airtight) and listed for wet locations.
- **Heated floor thermostat**: typically low-voltage; rough-in 48"–54" AFF at wall switch location.

## FRAMING AND BLOCKING

Before closing any wall, install ALL blocking needed for:
- **Grab bars**: horizontal 2×6 or 3/4" plywood backer at 33"–36" AFF on side walls (both sides of shower/tub); behind toilet at 33"–36" AFF on wall.
- **Shower niche**: frame opening between studs; add 2×4 blocking at top and bottom.
- **Towel bars, toilet paper holders**: blocking at rough heights (verify with fixture heights from design).
- **Vanity mirrors or medicine cabinets**: if recessed, verify cavity is between studs; block for secure mounting.

**Inspector will look for blocking during rough-in inspection — do not skip.**

## WATERPROOFING

### Tile Shower — Substrate
- Do NOT use standard drywall in wet areas, even moisture-resistant "greenboard."
- Correct substrates: cement board (Durock, HardieBacker), glass mat gypsum (DensShield), or foam board (Schluter KERDI-BOARD).
- Cement board must be set over a layer of unmodified thinset at studs.
- Joints in cement board: tape with alkali-resistant mesh tape and thinset; do NOT use paper tape.

### Tile Shower — Waterproof Membrane
- Apply RedGard, WEDI joint sealant, or Schluter Kerdi membrane over ALL cement board seams, corners, and fastener heads BEFORE tile.
- Full-coverage membrane (Schluter KERDI, USG Durock Tile Membrane): apply over entire shower walls and floor before tile.
- Floor pan liner: CPE or PVC liner must extend minimum 2" up wall studs; fold corners rather than cutting.
- Flood test before tile: plug drain, fill pan with 2" water, wait 24 hours, verify no loss.

### Tub Surround
- Cement board behind tile, extending minimum 1" above the tub ledge.
- Tub flange must be nailed to studs. Caulk between tile and tub with mold-resistant silicone (not grout).

## INSPECTION CHECKPOINTS

### Rough-In Inspection (before drywall)
- [ ] Plumbing: all drains and supplies installed and pressure-tested (100 PSI, 15 min on supply).
- [ ] Plumbing: all vents connected to stack.
- [ ] Toilet flange at correct height (at or slightly above future finished floor).
- [ ] Shower drain location correct for the pan specified.
- [ ] Electrical: GFCI locations correct, exhaust fan location, all circuits per plan.
- [ ] All blocking installed for grab bars, towel bars, fixtures.
- [ ] Shower niche framing complete.
- [ ] Waterproofing substrate: cement board installed correctly.
- [ ] Permit posted and inspection called.

### Pre-Tile Inspection
- [ ] Waterproof membrane applied and flood-tested (if applicable).
- [ ] All cement board joints taped.
- [ ] Floor leveled to within 3/8" in 10 feet.
- [ ] Drain height correct for tile thickness + thinset.

### Final Inspection
- [ ] All fixtures operational (toilet flushes, faucets on/off, shower valve tested).
- [ ] GFCI outlets test correctly.
- [ ] Exhaust fan venting to exterior confirmed.
- [ ] No leaks at any connection (run shower 5 min, check ceiling below).
- [ ] Grout and caulk complete (caulk at tile-to-tub and tile-to-floor transitions).
- [ ] Grab bars installed and torqued to blocking.
- [ ] All covers and trim plates installed.

## EVIDENCE REQUIRED FOR APPROVAL

- Photo: plumbing pressure test gauge (100 PSI before and after 15 min).
- Photo: toilet flange height relative to subfloor.
- Photo: blocking for grab bars in walls before closing.
- Photo: cement board installation showing correct overlap and fasteners.
- Photo: waterproof membrane coverage and flood test setup.
- Photo: GFCI outlet locations.
- Photo: exhaust fan duct routing to exterior termination.
- Photo: all rough-in with permit posted.
- Inspection card signed (rough-in + final).

## COMMON CHANGE ORDER TRIGGERS

| Discovery | Typical Scope | Cost Range |
|---|---|---|
| Subfloor rot under tub or toilet | Replace 4–16 sq ft subfloor | $400–$1,200 |
| No blocking in walls | Add blocking for grab bars/fixtures | $150–$400 |
| Mold behind tile | Remediation + replace board | $500–$3,000+ |
| Out-of-level floor | Self-leveling compound | $200–$600 |
| No exhaust fan duct to exterior | Add duct and exterior vent | $200–$500 |
| Wrong toilet flange height | Flange extender or rebuild | $150–$400 |
| Non-GFCI outlets | Replace or protect with upstream GFCI | $100–$250 |
| Improper shower drain rough-in | Re-rough drain for new pan spec | $300–$800 |

## PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS

- Every change order requires a written scope, price, and client signature before proceeding.
- Do not close walls until rough-in is inspected and documented.
- Flood test is not optional — it is the last opportunity to catch a leak before tile covers it.
- Grout is NOT waterproof. Silicone caulk (not grout) at all change-of-plane transitions (floor to wall, tub to tile, corner joints).
