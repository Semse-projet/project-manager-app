# Painting Work — SEMSE Trade Knowledge Guide

## Trade: painting
## Visibility: public_training

---

## SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

1. **Ventilation**: Oil-based paints, primers, and solvents produce VOCs. Work with windows open, fans exhausting out. Use NIOSH-approved respirator (OV/P100) for oil-based work in enclosed spaces.
2. **Lead paint**: Homes built before 1978 may contain lead paint. Do not sand, grind, or heat lead paint without EPA RRP (Renovation, Repair, and Painting) compliance. Contain work area, use HEPA vacuum.
3. **Fire hazard**: Oil-based paint rags can self-ignite through spontaneous combustion. Lay flat to dry outdoors or store in a sealed metal container with water.
4. **Ladder safety**: Use fiberglass ladders — aluminum conducts electricity. Maintain 3-point contact. Do not overreach — descend and move ladder.
5. **Skin and eye protection**: Safety glasses for overhead work. Chemical-resistant gloves for solvent cleanup.
6. **Pressure washer**: Eye protection required. Keep pressure washer wand away from windows, vents, and electrical outlets.

---

## CODE COMPLIANCE NOTES

- **Lead RRP Rule (EPA)**: If disturbing >6 sq ft of painted surface inside, or >20 sq ft outside in a pre-1978 home with children or pregnant women present, EPA RRP certification and compliance is required.
- **VOC limits**: Many jurisdictions limit VOC content in architectural coatings (SCAQMD Rule 1113; CARB rules). Use compliant products when required.
- **Fire-rated assemblies**: Do not apply non-fire-rated paints over fire-rated assemblies unless confirmed as paint-ready for that assembly. Some sprinkler covers require specific coatings.

## SURFACE PREPARATION (most important step)

### General Rule
A paint job is 80% prep and 20% application. A perfect application over bad prep will fail within 1–3 years.

### Interior Walls (Drywall)
1. Fill all nail holes, dents, and cracks with lightweight spackle or drywall compound.
2. Sand patches smooth when dry.
3. Clean walls with TSP or TSP substitute — removes grease, smoke, and grime.
4. Prime all patches and new drywall with drywall primer.
5. Sand primer lightly with 220-grit if raised grain is visible.

### Interior Trim and Doors
1. Clean with TSP. Sand with 120-grit to scuff existing finish.
2. Fill nail holes with wood filler. Allow to cure completely.
3. Prime bare wood and patched areas.
4. Sand primer smooth with 180-grit.
5. Fill any raised grain with thin application of primer-surfacer.

### Exterior Wood Siding
1. Power wash at 1200–1500 PSI (avoid excessive pressure near windows and joints).
2. Allow 48–72 hrs to dry (moisture content <15% before painting).
3. Scrape all loose paint with pull scraper, hook scraper.
4. Sand feather edges of scraped areas.
5. Spot prime all bare wood immediately — do not leave bare wood exposed to weather.
6. Fill cracks with paintable caulk (must be flexible — not spackling).
7. Caulk all gaps between siding and trim, around windows and doors, at all penetrations.

### Masonry/Concrete
1. Patch cracks with hydraulic cement or masonry patching compound.
2. Wire brush or grind efflorescence (white salt deposits).
3. Clean with muriatic acid solution (1:10) if heavy buildup — neutralize with baking soda wash.
4. Allow to dry and cure completely.
5. Apply masonry primer or block filler before topcoat.

## COMMON PROCEDURES

### Interior Painting — Standard Process
**Sequence: ceiling → walls → trim → doors**

1. Protect floors (canvas drop cloth, not plastic — plastic slides and doesn't absorb).
2. Remove outlet covers, switch plates, hardware.
3. Tape windows, trim, and ceiling perimeter with painter's tape (apply pressure to edges).
4. **Ceiling first**: cut in corners and perimeter with 2.5" angled brush, roll field with 3/4" nap roller.
5. **Walls**: cut in corners, ceiling line, and above baseboard. Roll field in W or M pattern, work wet edge.
6. **Trim**: use 2–2.5" angled sash brush, work with the grain. Two coats for coverage.
7. Remove tape while paint is still slightly wet (pull at 45° angle, back against itself).
8. Touch up after tape removal if needed.

### Spray Application (HVLP or Airless)
1. Mask everything not being painted — spray overspray travels 10 feet easily.
2. Thin paint per manufacturer spec (airless: usually no thinning; HVLP: 10–15%).
3. Keep gun 12" from surface. Move at consistent speed (1 ft/sec).
4. Overlap each pass by 50%.
5. Two thin coats are always better than one thick coat.
6. Clean gun immediately after use — clogged tips require soaking in solvent.

### Exterior Painting — Best Practices
1. Paint in temperatures between 50°F and 90°F, with humidity <85%.
2. Follow the sun: paint surfaces in shade — direct sun causes too-fast drying and lap marks.
3. Work top-down: soffits → fascia → walls → trim → foundation.
4. Back-brush or back-roll after spraying to ensure penetration.
5. Watch weather forecast: no painting if rain expected within 4 hours. No painting if temperature will drop below 50°F within 4 hours.

### Staining Decks and Wood
1. Clean with deck wash (oxygenated bleach cleaner) and power rinse.
2. Allow to dry 48–72 hours.
3. Apply stain with brush, roller, or pump sprayer — immediately back-brush to work into grain.
4. Solid stains: 2 coats. Semi-transparent: 1–2 coats max (more won't absorb, will peel).
5. Transparent stains: reapply annually or when water stops beading.

## PAINT SELECTION GUIDE

### Interior
| Location | Finish | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Ceilings | Flat | Hides imperfections |
| Living areas (walls) | Eggshell or Matte | Washable, low sheen |
| Kitchens, baths (walls) | Satin | Moisture-resistant, washable |
| Trim, doors, cabinets | Semi-gloss or Gloss | Hard, washable, durable |
| High-traffic areas | Satin or Semi-gloss | Scrubbable |

### Exterior
| Surface | Product | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Wood siding | 100% acrylic latex | Flexible, breathable |
| Hardboard siding | 100% acrylic latex | Prime all cut edges |
| Trim | Acrylic or oil-based | Oil = harder, slower dry |
| Masonry/stucco | Elastomeric or masonry paint | Bridges hairline cracks |
| Metal (doors, rails) | DTM (direct to metal) primer + topcoat | Rust inhibiting |
| Decks | Deck paint or solid stain | Designed for foot traffic |

## INSPECTION CHECKPOINTS

### Before Painting
- [ ] All surfaces clean, dry, and properly primed.
- [ ] All cracks and holes filled and sanded smooth.
- [ ] All caulk applied and fully cured (24 hrs).
- [ ] All masking/drop cloths in place.
- [ ] Client-approved paint colors confirmed.

### After Painting
- [ ] Coverage: no holidays (missed spots) visible in raking light.
- [ ] No runs, sags, or drips.
- [ ] Crisp paint lines at tape edges, ceiling/wall junctions, trim.
- [ ] No lap marks or roller texture inconsistency.
- [ ] All hardware and covers reinstalled.
- [ ] Clean: no paint on glass, hardware, floors.

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Skipping primer**: New drywall, repaired patches, stain-blocking areas, and bare wood all require primer. Paint alone will not provide adhesion or uniformity.
2. **Painting over dirty or glossy surfaces**: Paint won't bond. Always clean and scuff-sand glossy surfaces.
3. **Cheap roller nap for smooth walls**: Use 3/8" nap for smooth surfaces, 1/2" for medium texture, 3/4" for rough texture. Cheap rollers leave lint on walls.
4. **Leaving tape on too long**: Masking tape left overnight or exposed to sun becomes difficult to remove and tears paint. Remove while still slightly wet.
5. **One coat on bare wood or new drywall**: Always two finish coats minimum for durability.
6. **Painting in cold or wet conditions**: Paint won't cure properly below 50°F or above 85% humidity. Will peel within a year.
7. **Not caulking before painting exterior**: Gaps behind trim allow water infiltration causing rot and paint failure within 2–3 years.
8. **Rolling ceiling against wet wall paint**: Creates "holiday" overlap marks. Paint ceiling first, let dry, then cut in at ceiling line on walls.

## TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

### Paint peeling off wall
- On new drywall: skipped primer — reprime and repaint.
- On previously painted wall: poor adhesion from dirty or glossy surface — strip, clean, scuff, prime, repaint.
- On exterior: moisture from behind — find and fix moisture source before repainting.

### Runs and sags
- Cause: too much paint applied at once (spraying too slow or too close, roller overloaded).
- Fix while wet: brush out immediately. After drying: sand smooth, repaint with thinner coats.

### Lap marks on walls
- Cause: working a dried edge. Roll wet-to-wet by maintaining a wet edge across the wall.
- Fix: sand lightly and repaint the entire wall panel.

### Paint won't stick to trim
- Cause: silicone caulk residue, wax, or paint release (paint filmed over oil-based without dewaxing).
- Fix: clean with solvent, lightly sand, prime with shellac-based primer (BIN), repaint.

### Stains bleeding through paint
- Cause: nicotine, water stains, smoke damage, or knots in wood.
- Fix: spot prime with shellac-based primer (BIN or Zinsser) or pigmented shellac. Let dry, repaint.
