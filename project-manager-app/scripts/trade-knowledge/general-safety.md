## UNIVERSAL JOB SITE SAFETY RULES

These rules apply regardless of trade and should be followed on every SEMSE job:

1. **Identify ALL utilities before any excavation or penetration**: Call 811 (Dig Safe) minimum 3 business days before digging. Mark gas, electric, water, sewer, and telecom lines.
2. **Emergency numbers posted**: First aid kit location, nearest hospital, and 911 must be known by every worker on site.
3. **PPE baseline**: Safety glasses, hard-toed boots, and hard hat on every active construction site. Gloves at discretion by task.
4. **No alcohol or drugs**: Zero tolerance. Anyone under impairment is removed from site immediately.
5. **Housekeeping**: Clean site is a safe site. Trip hazards (cords, debris, material offcuts) must be cleared continuously. Designate a debris area and dump it daily.
6. **Tool inspection**: Inspect all tools before use. Damaged cords, cracked guards, or missing safety features → remove from service immediately.
7. **Securing work area**: During work hours, barricade the job zone from public access. After hours, secure site against unauthorized entry.

## OSHA REQUIREMENTS FOR RESIDENTIAL CONSTRUCTION

### Fall Protection (OSHA 1926 Subpart M)
- Required when working at or above 6 feet on residential construction, 4 feet in general industry.
- Options: guardrail system, personal fall arrest system (PFAS/harness), or safety net.
- Leading edges, floor holes, and roof edges must be protected or covered.
- Floor hole covers: must support 200 lbs, be secured, and be labeled "HOLE" or "COVER."
- Ladders must extend 3 feet above landing. Set at 4:1 angle (1 foot out for every 4 feet up).

### Scaffolding (OSHA 1926 Subpart L)
- Must support 4× its maximum intended load.
- Fully planked or decked work surface.
- Guardrail required when scaffold is 10 feet or higher (residential).
- Erected by a competent person.
- Inspected before each shift.

### Hazard Communication (OSHA 1910.1200 — HazCom / GHS)
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) must be accessible for all hazardous chemicals on site.
- Containers must be labeled with product name, pictograms, and hazard statements.
- Workers must be trained on SDS use and hazard identification.

### Electrical Safety (OSHA 1926 Subpart K)
- Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters (GFCI) required on all 120V power tools during construction.
- Extension cords: use only grounded 3-wire cords. Never use indoor cords outdoors.
- Temporary wiring must be inspected by competent person.
- Cord damage (cut, frayed, exposed copper): remove from service.

## PERMITS AND INSPECTIONS

### When a Permit is Required (typical — verify with local AHJ)
- New construction, additions, or alterations.
- Structural work: any framing, beam replacement, load path changes.
- Electrical work: new circuits, panel work, service upgrades.
- Plumbing: new rough-in, water heater replacement, drain line work.
- HVAC: new equipment, duct work, gas line work.
- **Not typically required**: painting, flooring, cabinet replacement, fixture swaps (no rough-in changes).

### Permit Process
1. Obtain permit before starting work.
2. Post permit and approved plans at job site — inspector must see them.
3. Schedule inspections at the required stages (rough-in BEFORE covering work).
4. Final inspection required before occupancy.
5. Close permit within the jurisdiction's deadline (typically 6–24 months).

### Consequences of Skipping Permits
- Insurance may deny claims for work performed without permits.
- Buyer's home inspection will flag unpermitted work — kills sale or requires remediation.
- Local AHJ can require demolition and rebuild of unpermitted work.
- Contractor's license can be suspended or revoked.

## LEAD AND ASBESTOS AWARENESS

### Lead Paint (homes built before 1978)
- Assume lead is present until tested.
- Visual inspection: peeling, chalking, or chipping paint in pre-1978 homes.
- XRF testing: best method, requires certified inspector.
- EPA RRP Rule: if disturbing >6 sq ft interior / >20 sq ft exterior in pre-1978 home where children or pregnant women may be present:
  - Use EPA-certified firm.
  - Apply lead-safe work practices.
  - Containment, wet methods, HEPA vacuum.
  - Document and provide lead disclosure to occupant.

### Asbestos (homes built before 1980)
- Common locations: floor tiles (9"×9" vinyl), popcorn ceilings, pipe insulation, duct insulation, roofing felt, some exterior siding (transite).
- If suspect material found: STOP, do not disturb.
- Hire certified asbestos inspector to test.
- Abatement: by licensed asbestos contractor only. Encapsulation or removal per EPA and state regulations.
- Disposal: sealed, labeled bags, licensed landfill only.

## DOCUMENTATION AND PHOTO EVIDENCE STANDARDS

### Required at Every Job
- **Before photos**: document existing conditions at start. Protects against false damage claims.
- **Progress photos**: rough-in stage before covering. Documents code compliance.
- **After photos**: completed work. Used for client approval and marketing.
- **Permit and inspection photos**: inspection card signed by inspector, permit posted.

### Photo Best Practices
- Always include a timestamp (phone camera metadata is fine).
- Use a reference object for scale (tape measure, hard hat) when photographing details.
- Take wide shots (full room/area) and close-up shots (connection details, compliance items).
- Store on cloud with job name and date folder structure.
- Never delete original photos — they may be needed for warranty or dispute resolution.

## JOB CLOSEOUT CHECKLIST

Before releasing final payment or requesting final inspection, verify:

**Site Condition**
- [ ] All debris removed and job site swept clean.
- [ ] All tools and equipment removed.
- [ ] All temporary protection (plastic sheeting, plywood covers) removed.
- [ ] No materials left on-site unless approved for future phases.

**Work Quality**
- [ ] All work matches contract scope — nothing added or omitted without change order.
- [ ] All rough-in inspections passed.
- [ ] Final inspection passed and permit closed.
- [ ] Punch list items completed (document with photos).

**Documentation**
- [ ] All warranties transferred to client (equipment, materials, workmanship).
- [ ] All owner's manuals and equipment documentation provided to client.
- [ ] All permit and inspection cards provided or copies given.
- [ ] All change orders documented and signed.

**Client Communication**
- [ ] Client walkthrough completed before final payment.
- [ ] Operation of new systems explained (thermostat, appliances, shutoffs).
- [ ] Maintenance items communicated (filter changes, annual tune-ups, caulk inspection).

## PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT STANDARDS

- **Communicate proactively**: notify client of any schedule changes, scope changes, or unforeseen conditions immediately. Never let surprises happen at invoice time.
- **No unauthorized substitutions**: do not substitute materials without written client approval.
- **Subcontractor management**: you are responsible for your subs. Ensure they are licensed, insured, and following safe work practices.
- **Change orders**: never start out-of-scope work without a signed change order. Verbal agreements protect no one.
- **License and insurance**: maintain current contractor's license, general liability, and workers' compensation coverage at all times.
- **Lien rights**: if payment is delayed beyond contract terms, follow proper preliminary notice and lien procedures for your state.
