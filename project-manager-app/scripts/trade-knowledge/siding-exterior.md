## OVERVIEW — WATER MANAGEMENT PRINCIPLE

Every decision in siding work follows one principle: **water that gets behind the siding must be able to drain out without touching the structure.** The wall is a drainage plane, not a barrier. If you understand this, you will avoid every major siding failure mode.

**Correct sequence:**
1. Remove old siding → 2. Inspect/repair sheathing → 3. Install WRB → 4. Install window/door flashings → 5. Install starter strip and corner treatments → 6. Install siding bottom-to-top → 7. Install trim → 8. Caulk penetrations → 9. Paint/prime

---

## WALL PREPARATION

### Removing Existing Siding
- Check for asbestos (pre-1980 cement fiber siding/"transite" siding) before cutting or power-washing — test first.
- Remove all old flashing, caulk residue, and any deteriorated trim.
- After removal, inspect sheathing for damage, rot, and fastener integrity.
- Photograph all sheathing condition before covering. This is the baseline for any hidden condition dispute.

### Sheathing Repair
- Rot or damage: replace affected panels. Do not install siding over compromised sheathing.
- OSB sheathing exposed for more than 2 weeks: check for edge swelling. Grind or plane swollen edges before WRB and siding.
- Missing or loose fasteners: refasten with ring-shank nails or screws into studs. WRB and siding only perform as well as the sheathing they're attached to.
- Minimum sheathing for vinyl siding: 3/8" OSB or plywood (for nail holding). Some manufacturers require 1/2" OSB.

## WEATHER-RESISTIVE BARRIER (WRB) — HOUSE WRAP

### Material Options
- **Standard 1-layer house wrap** (Tyvek HomeWrap, Typar): adequate for most climates, but drains slowly.
- **Drainage mat house wrap** (Tyvek DrainWrap, Jumbo Tex): dimpled or textured to create drainage gap between WRB and siding. Recommended in high-rain climates.
- **Felt paper (15-lb or 30-lb)**: traditional, still code-allowed; must be lapped correctly; deteriorates in UV exposure if not covered quickly.
- **Fluid-applied WRB** (Henry Blueskin, STO Guard): sprayed or roller-applied. Best air sealing. Requires primer coat on OSB.

### Installation
- Start at bottom, work up. Each row laps over the row below by minimum 6" (horizontal laps).
- Vertical laps: minimum 12", in direction of prevailing wind or toward the interior (lap so water runs outside).
- Wrap around corners: extend WRB minimum 12" around corners.
- Fasten with plastic-cap roofing nails or house wrap staples every 12–18" horizontally, every 6"–8" at vertical framing.
- **Around windows and doors**: follow "I" flashing method (see windows-doors guide). Sill pan flashing, then jamb WRB, then head WRB on top.
- **All seams and tears**: tape with WRB-compatible flashing tape (3M 8067 All Weather Flashing Tape, Huber ZIP Tape). Do not leave unsealed tears — air leaks dramatically affect thermal performance and moisture management.

## FLASHINGS — CRITICAL INTEGRATION POINTS

### At Windows and Doors
- See windows-doors guide. Siding installs OVER and AFTER window flashing — never before.
- Siding must be cut back 1/4" from window casing on head and jambs.
- Leave 1/4" gap between siding butt edge and window casing — bridged by caulk only at head and jambs, NOT at sill (drainage).

### At Roof-Wall Intersections (Step Flashing)
- Step flashing: individual L-shaped metal pieces, one per shingle course.
- Each step flashing is laid on top of a shingle, then covered by the next shingle — weaved in, not laid on top of shingles.
- Siding installs over step flashing — not under it. Overlap step flashing by 2".
- At kickout flashing (bottom of roof-wall intersection): kickout directs water away from wall. This is the #1 missed flashing item and causes severe water damage behind siding.

### At Penetrations (Pipes, Vents, Electrical)
- Use round pipe flashing boots over all pipe penetrations.
- Integrate boot flashing tape into WRB: bottom first, then sides, then top (so water can't enter behind boot).
- Seal around boot with appropriate sealant for pipe material.

### At Grade (Bottom of Wall)
- Siding must terminate minimum 6" above grade (IBC) or 8" above grade (many local codes and manufacturer requirements).
- Finish grade must slope away from foundation: minimum 6" drop in first 10 feet.
- Install pressure-treated 2×4 sleeper at base of wall if siding needs to terminate lower than 6" for aesthetic reasons — separate the siding from the wet zone.

## STARTER STRIP AND CORNERS

### Vinyl Siding
- Starter strip: nailed along bottom course line, level across the full wall.
- J-channel: installed at all windows, doors, corners, and penetrations to receive siding ends.
- Outside corner posts: plumb, nailed every 8–10".
- Inside corner posts (or J-channel backed with another J-channel).

### Fiber Cement Siding (HardiPlank, Allura)
- No starter strip — bottom course nailed directly over WRB.
- Trim boards first: install corner trim, window and door trim before field siding.
- Minimum 1" clearance from bottom course to finished grade (manufacturer requirement + IRC requirement).
- Pre-prime all cut edges immediately — fiber cement wicks water at cuts and will swell and delaminate if unprimed.

### Wood Siding (Cedar, Pine)
- Pre-prime all 4 sides before installation. This is the single biggest factor in paint life.
- Starter strip or kick-out backer: even thickness, level.
- Bottom course: expose about 1" more than standard reveal (thicker butt) for visual balance.

## SIDING INSTALLATION

### Fastening — General Rules
- **Do not overdrive fasteners**: siding must be able to move with thermal expansion. Nails should be "hand-tight" with 1/32" gap between nail head and siding face — especially on vinyl.
- **Nail into framing**: siding nails must penetrate into stud framing, not just sheathing. Minimum 1.25" penetration into framing.
- **Nail type**: stainless steel ring-shank preferred (won't rust, won't pull out). Aluminum acceptable for aluminum siding. Never use non-galvanized steel nails (rust stains).

### Vinyl Siding
- Lock bottom of panel into course below, slide panel into corner post and J-channel.
- Nail every 16" through slot (not through face). Center nail in slot — do not nail tight.
- Expansion gap: leave 1/4" gap at each end inside J-channel.
- Lap vertical seams minimum 1" and away from primary viewpoint.
- Temperature: do not install below 40°F (becomes brittle) or above 100°F (pre-bowed).

### Fiber Cement (HardiPlank)
- Minimum 1.25" ring-shank hot-dipped galvanized nails or screws.
- Nail 1" from ends; nail per course into each stud.
- Lap: 1.25" minimum overlap.
- Joints: butt joints must land on a stud. Caulk butt joints with HardieFlex sealant (flexible, paintable).
- Prime cut ends immediately before covering with trim.
- Maintain manufacturer-required clearances from rooflines, decks, and grade.

### Wood Siding (Bevel/Clapboard)
- Face-nail into studs with ring-shank nails (2 nails per stud per course for wide boards).
- Set nail slightly below surface, fill before painting.
- Maintain consistent reveal using a siding gauge or layout stick.
- Caulk all butt joints and penetrations before painting.

## MOISTURE MANAGEMENT — CRITICAL DETAILS

### Weep Screeds and Drainage
- Vinyl, fiber cement, and wood siding are all drainage systems — water WILL get behind them.
- Bottom of each wall must have a way to drain: weep holes in starter strip (vinyl), open face at bottom course (fiber cement), open gap at starter strip (wood).
- Never caulk the bottom of the siding course — blocks drainage.

### Rainscreen Gap
- In high-rain climates (>20" annual rain), consider installing a 1×4 or drainage mat as a "rainscreen" between WRB and siding. Creates a drained cavity that dramatically reduces moisture loading on siding.
- Rainscreen adds cost but prevents virtually all siding moisture failures.

## INSPECTION CHECKLIST

### Before Siding Begins
- [ ] Sheathing condition documented (photos before WRB).
- [ ] WRB installed, all seams taped, no tears.
- [ ] Window and door flashings integrated with WRB.
- [ ] Kickout flashings installed at all roof-wall intersections.
- [ ] Starter strip or bottom trim level across full wall.

### During Installation
- [ ] Fasteners not overdriven (vinyl: gap visible).
- [ ] Courses level (check every 5–6 courses with a 4-foot level).
- [ ] Seams staggered (not aligned vertically from course to course).
- [ ] Expansion gaps maintained at corners and channels (vinyl).
- [ ] Cut edges primed (fiber cement).

### Final
- [ ] All penetrations sealed with appropriate flashing/sealant.
- [ ] All j-channel and corner posts plumb.
- [ ] Bottom of siding minimum 6" above grade.
- [ ] No siding touching the roof surface.
- [ ] Caulk at all butt joints (wood, fiber cement) — but NOT at bottom sill (drainage).
- [ ] All trim installed and caulked.
- [ ] First coat of paint/primer applied (if unpainted product).

## COMMON FAILURES AND DISPUTE PREVENTION

| Failure | Root Cause | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Water damage behind siding | Missed kickout flashing | Install kickout at every roof-wall intersection |
| Siding cracks/splits in winter (vinyl) | Nailed too tight, no expansion gap | Never nail tight; leave 1/4" in channels |
| Delaminating fiber cement at butt joints | Cut ends not primed | Prime all cuts immediately |
| Rust stains on siding | Non-galvanized or zinc nails | Use stainless or hot-dipped galvanized only |
| Water entry at window head | No drip cap under WRB | WRB must fold over drip cap |
| Mold inside wall | WRB lapped wrong (water trapped) | WRB always laps upper over lower |
| Siding touching ground (rot) | Installed too low | Maintain 6"–8" clearance from grade |
