# Drywall Work — SEMSE Trade Knowledge Guide

## Trade: drywall
## Visibility: public_training

---

## SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

1. **Respiratory protection**: Drywall compound dust contains crystalline silica. Wear N95 minimum during sanding; P100 respirator for heavy sanding.
2. **Eye protection**: Safety glasses or goggles at all times during cutting and sanding.
3. **Joint compound hazard**: Premixed compound contains biocides. Wash hands thoroughly. Do not allow to dry on skin.
4. **Ladder safety**: Use proper stepladder or scaffolding for ceiling work. Never stand on top of stepladder.
5. **Electrical awareness**: Before cutting drywall, scan wall with a stud finder that has wire and pipe detection mode. Shut off circuits if cutting near outlet locations.
6. **Heavy board handling**: 5/8" drywall is 2.2 lbs per sq ft (70 lbs per 4×8 sheet). Use panel lift for ceilings. Two-person lift minimum for 12-foot sheets.

## CODE COMPLIANCE CHECKPOINTS

### Fire-Rated Assemblies
- **1-hour fire wall** (garage-to-living space): 5/8" Type X drywall on garage side. Tape and mud not required for fire rating, but all penetrations must be fire-stopped.
- **2-hour fire wall**: Double layer of 5/8" Type X.
- Garage ceiling below living space: 5/8" Type X required (IRC R302.6).
- All penetrations through fire-rated assemblies must be sealed with fire-rated caulk or putty pads.

### Moisture-Resistant Drywall Requirements
- Bathrooms (behind tile): use cement board (Durock, HardieBacker) or glass mat gypsum (DensShield, GreenBoard) — **not** standard paper-faced green board behind wet areas.
- Shower surrounds: cement board only, properly waterproofed.
- Kitchen backsplash areas: moisture-resistant drywall acceptable.
- Exterior soffits: use moisture-resistant or exterior-rated gypsum product.

### Screw Spacing
- Edge screws: 8" on center along tapered edges.
- Field screws: 12" on center in the field.
- Ceiling: 12" on center max; reduce to 8" if truss uplift is a concern.
- Screws must be dimpled (set below surface) but not break the paper face.

## COMMON PROCEDURES

### Hanging Drywall on Walls
1. Locate and mark all stud locations on floor and ceiling.
2. Start at a corner. Orient panels horizontally (strongest installation).
3. First course: leave 1/2" gap at floor (moisture buffer — will be covered by baseboard).
4. Measure and cut panels: score face with utility knife, snap, cut back paper.
5. Lift and hold panel tight to ceiling (use foot lifter). Screw 8" OC on edges, 12" OC in field.
6. Stagger vertical seams — never align seams on adjacent rows.
7. Cut outlets and switch boxes: measure location precisely, use drywall router or jab saw.
8. All seams must land on a stud or blocking — never leave a seam floating.

### Hanging Drywall on Ceilings
1. Use a panel lift — do not attempt 12-foot ceilings by hand.
2. Run panels perpendicular to joists.
3. First panel in corner, work across.
4. Screw at 12" OC into every joist.
5. Add blocking (2×4 between joists) if a seam falls between joists.

### Taping and Mudding (3-coat system)
**Coat 1 — Taping coat (setting compound or all-purpose)**
- Apply compound to all joints with 6" knife.
- Embed paper tape: center over joint, press firmly, smooth. No bubbles.
- Coat all screw dimples.
- Inside corners: fold tape, apply compound to both sides.
- Outside corners: install metal or vinyl corner bead, coat with compound.
- Let dry completely (minimum 24 hrs at 70°F, 50% RH). Do not force-dry with heat gun — causes cracking.

**Coat 2 — Filler coat (all-purpose or topping compound)**
- Sand only the high spots from coat 1 — do not sand to bare tape.
- Apply with 10" knife, feathering 2–3 inches wider on each side.
- Fill screw dimples again.
- Let dry completely.

**Coat 3 — Finish coat (topping compound)**
- Sand coat 2 smooth (120-grit).
- Apply thin skim coat with 12" knife.
- Feather 2–3 inches wider than coat 2.
- Let dry completely.

**Sanding**
- Hand sand or pole sand with 120-grit.
- Finish with 150-grit for smooth surface.
- Wipe with barely damp cloth to remove dust before priming.
- Prime with drywall primer BEFORE painting — bare mud will flash (show through paint).

### Patching a Hole (6" or smaller)
1. Cut hole into a clean square or circle.
2. California patch: cut piece slightly larger, score back paper and peel, leaving 2" paper border.
3. Apply compound to wall, press patch in, smooth border paper with more compound.
4. Two finish coats, sand, prime, paint.

### Patching a Large Hole (over 6")
1. Cut opening to nearest studs on left and right.
2. Add horizontal blocking (2×4) at top and bottom of opening.
3. Install new drywall, tape seams with standard 3-coat process.

## INSPECTION CHECKPOINTS

### Before Taping
- [ ] All mechanicals (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) rough-in inspected and approved.
- [ ] All blocking installed for future grab bars (bathrooms), TV mounts, heavy shelving.
- [ ] Fire-rated Type X installed in correct locations (garage, stairwell, furnace room).
- [ ] Moisture-resistant board in wet areas.
- [ ] No floating seams — all edges on framing.
- [ ] Insulation installed and inspected.

### Before Painting (after tape and mud)
- [ ] All seams invisible under raking light.
- [ ] All screw dimples filled and smooth.
- [ ] Outside corners straight and tight.
- [ ] Inside corners clean and not cracked.
- [ ] No bubbles in tape.
- [ ] No ridges or high spots in field.
- [ ] Drywall primer applied.

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Hanging drywall vertically on walls**: Horizontal installation creates stronger walls — seams hit at mid-height where studs provide support.
2. **Cracking tape on inside corners**: Apply compound to both sides of corner before folding and pressing tape. Dry-pressing causes lifting.
3. **Over-sanding into tape**: Sanding through the paper face of tape creates a weak spot that will crack. Sand only the ridges, not the surface.
4. **Mud too thick in one coat**: Each coat should be thin. Thick coats shrink and crack as they dry.
5. **Skipping primer**: Unpainted drywall will absorb paint unevenly, showing flashing (flat vs. shiny spots). Always prime first.
6. **No gap at floor**: Drywall touching a concrete slab will wick moisture and mold. Always maintain 1/2" gap.
7. **Seams aligned vertically**: Stagger seams between rows — never create a continuous vertical seam from floor to ceiling.
8. **Screws breaking paper face**: Paper-faced screws have no holding power. Adjust screw gun depth and recheck.

## TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

### Tape bubbling or lifting
- Cause: insufficient compound under tape, or tape applied over dry compound.
- Fix: cut out bubbled section with utility knife, re-tape with fresh compound.

### Cracks at inside corners
- Cause: seasonal movement (wood framing expands/contracts). Also caused by too-thick compound.
- Fix: apply flexible tape (fiber mesh) inside corners, or use paintable caulk instead of compound on inside corners.

### Nail pops or screw pops
- Cause: framing shrinkage pushes fasteners out after drywall is hung.
- Fix: drive a new screw 2" above and below the pop, dimple the popped fastener, fill all three with compound.

### Seams showing through paint
- Cause: skipped primer, or insufficient mudding (only 1 or 2 coats).
- Fix: sand, apply additional compound coats, sand, prime, repaint.

### Joint edges cracking after painting
- Cause: compound applied too thick, dried too fast, or moisture issue.
- Fix: cut the crack, apply fresh compound, re-feather.

## DRYWALL TYPES QUICK REFERENCE

| Type | Thickness | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | 1/2" | Interior walls, most applications |
| Standard | 5/8" | Ceilings (sag-resistant), fire-rated assemblies |
| Type X | 5/8" | Fire-rated walls (garage, mechanical rooms) |
| Moisture-Resistant (MR) | 1/2" | Bathrooms (non-wet areas), kitchens |
| Cement Board | 1/2", 5/8" | Tile substrate in wet areas |
| Soundboard | 1/2" | Sound attenuation (between rooms) |
| Lightweight | 1/2" | Easier handling, slightly less impact resistance |
