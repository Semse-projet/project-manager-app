# Plumbing Work — SEMSE Trade Knowledge Guide

## Trade: plumbing
## Visibility: public_training

---

## SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

1. **Shut off water supply**: Locate and close the main shutoff valve before cutting any pipe. Know where secondary shutoffs are (under sink, behind toilet, at water heater).
2. **Drain the line**: Open the lowest faucet in the house to drain pressure. Open an upstairs faucet to let air in and drain faster.
3. **Hot water caution**: Water heater stored at 120°F. Allow hot water lines to cool before cutting.
4. **Gas line awareness**: Know if gas supply lines run near your work area. If you smell gas: stop, ventilate, call the gas company.
5. **Lead pipe identification**: Pre-1986 homes may have lead supply pipes (gray, soft, slightly shiny). Do not disturb without proper handling.
6. **Sewage exposure**: Use nitrile gloves, eye protection, and N95 mask when working on drain lines. Wash hands thoroughly.

## CODE COMPLIANCE CHECKPOINTS (UPC / IPC 2021)

### Supply Lines
- Minimum pressure at fixtures: 15 PSI static. Maximum: 80 PSI (pressure regulator required above 80 PSI).
- Water hammer arrestors required at washing machines and quick-close valves (ASSE 1010).
- Hot water supply: anti-scald mixing valves required at showers and tubs (ASSE 1016/1070), set to max 120°F.
- Cross-connection protection: vacuum breakers required on hose bibs, irrigation systems, and ice maker lines.
- Pipe materials allowed: copper (Types L and K), PEX (A, B, C), CPVC, galvanized steel (supply), PP-R.
- PVC is NOT allowed for hot water supply lines.

### Drain-Waste-Vent (DWV)
- Every fixture must have a P-trap with a minimum 2" seal.
- Trap arm length: max 24" for 1.5" drain; max 36" for 2" drain (varies by code).
- Vent pipes must terminate at least 6 inches above the roof flashing; 12" in snow regions.
- Vent stack must be minimum 3" diameter where it exits the roof.
- Wet venting allowed for bathroom groups (toilet + lav + tub) in specific configurations.
- No double-trap on a single fixture — one trap per fixture, period.
- All horizontal drain lines must slope: minimum 1/4" per foot for 3" and smaller; 1/8" per foot for 4" and larger.
- Cleanout required at base of each stack, at changes of direction over 45°, and every 100 feet on horizontal runs.

### Fixture Rough-In Dimensions (standard)
- Toilet: flange center 12" from finished wall (15" for ADA), 15" min from centerline to side wall.
- Lavatory: drain center typically 17–20" from wall, 18" min height from finished floor to rim.
- Shower: drain 60" from wall, floor sloped 1/4" per foot toward drain, minimum 36" × 36" enclosure (32" min in remodels).
- Bathtub: drain location varies by tub model — verify before framing.

## COMMON PROCEDURES

### Replacing a P-Trap
1. Place bucket under trap. Loosen slip nuts by hand or channel-lock pliers.
2. Remove old trap and clean pipe ends.
3. Install new trap with slip joints — hand-tight plus 1/4 turn. Do not overtighten PVC (cracks).
4. Run water, check for leaks at all joints for 2 minutes.

### Sweat-Soldering Copper Pipe
1. Cut pipe square with a pipe cutter. Ream the inside to remove burr.
2. Clean pipe end and fitting interior with emery cloth until shiny.
3. Apply flux to both surfaces.
4. Heat fitting with torch — apply heat to the fitting, not the pipe.
5. When flux sizzles, touch lead-free solder to the joint (opposite side from flame) — it should flow in by capillary action.
6. Let cool 60 seconds before moving. Wipe with damp rag.
7. After cooling, test joint by turning water on slowly and checking for any seeping.

### Installing PEX Supply Lines
1. Cut PEX square with PEX cutter.
2. Expansion PEX (PEX-A): use expansion tool to expand pipe end, slide fitting in, ring contracts and seals within 30 seconds.
3. Crimp PEX (PEX-B/C): slide crimp ring onto pipe, insert fitting, position ring 1/8–1/4" from end, crimp with go/no-go gauge.
4. Clamp (cinch) PEX: slide clamp ring, use cinch tool to tighten.
5. Minimum bend radius: 8× pipe diameter. Use elbow fittings for tight turns.
6. Support PEX every 32" horizontally; every 6 feet vertically.
7. PEX must not be exposed to UV — protect in exterior or garage applications.

### Setting a Toilet
1. Install new wax ring on flange horn (or on toilet horn — one method, not both).
2. Lower toilet straight down over flange bolts — do not slide sideways once wax contacts.
3. Press down firmly and sit on toilet to compress wax.
4. Install brass washers and nuts — hand-tight, then 1/4 turn. Do not overtighten (cracks the porcelain).
5. Install supply line: hand-tight at tank, then 1/4 turn at angle stop.
6. Flush 3 times, check base for leaks, check tank-to-bowl connection.

## INSPECTION CHECKPOINTS

### Rough-In Inspection
- [ ] All drain lines sloped correctly (1/4" per foot visible).
- [ ] All vent pipes connected to stack or individual vents to roof.
- [ ] All supply lines pressure-tested (100 PSI for 15 minutes, no drop).
- [ ] Stub-outs capped and located at correct rough-in dimensions.
- [ ] Water heater location approved (pan installed, relief valve piped to within 6" of floor).
- [ ] No drain cleanouts blocked by framing.
- [ ] All pipe penetrations through fire-rated assemblies fire-stopped.

### Final Inspection
- [ ] All fixtures installed and operational.
- [ ] No leaks at any connection — supply, drain, or vent.
- [ ] Anti-scald valves tested at shower/tub (max 120°F at outlet).
- [ ] Pressure reducing valve set to 60–80 PSI.
- [ ] Water heater set to 120°F, T&P relief valve piped correctly.
- [ ] All cleanouts accessible and capped.
- [ ] Hose bibs have vacuum breakers.

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **No vent or improper vent**: Causes siphoning of P-traps, sewer gas enters home. Every fixture needs a properly sized vent.
2. **Trap too far from vent**: Trap arm exceeds code limits — drain gurgles and trap siphons.
3. **Improper slope**: Drain pitched too steep (>1/2" per foot) causes solids to separate from liquid and creates blockages. Too little slope causes settling.
4. **Solder joints with water present**: Even a trickle prevents solder from flowing. Stuff bread in the pipe to absorb moisture or use a pipe freeze kit.
5. **Over-tightening plastic fittings**: PVC and ABS slip joints only need hand-tight + 1/4 turn. Over-tightening cracks fittings or distorts seats.
6. **No expansion gap for PEX**: PEX expands ~1" per 10°F change per 100 feet. Allow for expansion at manifolds and long runs.
7. **Mixing incompatible metals without dielectric union**: Copper to galvanized steel causes galvanic corrosion. Use a dielectric union.
8. **Double trap**: Never install two traps in series on one fixture. Creates seal-breaking air pockets.

## TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

### Drain is slow
- First: snake the fixture drain (20–25 feet) before assuming a main line clog.
- If multiple fixtures slow simultaneously: main line blockage — use 3" or 4" snake from cleanout.
- Persistent slow drains with good slope: often inadequate venting — check vent stack.

### Sewer gas smell indoors
- First: find dried-out P-traps (pour water in floor drains and infrequently used sinks).
- Check wax ring at toilet (movement of toilet breaks seal).
- Check vent stack for blockage (dead animal, leaves, ice cap in winter).

### Water hammer (banging pipes)
- Install water hammer arrestor at washing machine and quick-close valves.
- Check water pressure — reduce to 60 PSI if above 80 PSI with PRV.

### Leak at soldered joint
- Heat joint again to melt solder, clean, re-flux, and re-solder.
- If water present, use repair coupling or push-to-connect SharkBite as temporary fix.

### No hot water (tank water heater)
- Gas: check pilot light, thermocouple, gas supply valve open.
- Electric: check breaker (usually 240V, 30A), reset high-limit switch on element.
- Both: T&P valve may have opened and drained the tank — check floor drain near heater.
