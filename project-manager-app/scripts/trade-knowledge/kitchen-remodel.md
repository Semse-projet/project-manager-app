## OVERVIEW — TRADE DEPENDENCIES

Kitchen remodels are the highest-value and highest-complexity residential job. Multiple trades must sequence correctly or rework cascades. An uncoordinated kitchen costs 15–25% more than a coordinated one.

**Correct sequence:**
1. Demolition + protection → 2. Rough plumbing → 3. Rough electrical → 4. HVAC/range hood rough → 5. Framing/blocking → 6. Drywall/patching → 7. Cabinets → 8. Countertop template → 9. Countertop install → 10. Tile/flooring → 11. Plumbing fixtures → 12. Appliance install → 13. Final electrical → 14. Punch list

---

## DEMOLITION AND PROTECTION

1. **Utility shutoffs first**: Main water off if removing sink; circuit breakers for all kitchen circuits off. Label breakers at panel.
2. **Adjacent protection**: Refrigerator, oven, and dishwasher must be disconnected and moved before demo. Protect hardwood floors in adjacent rooms with masonite sheets.
3. **Cabinet removal order**: Upper cabinets first, then base cabinets. Upper cabinets are heavy — two-person job minimum. Have a helper hold while another removes screws.
4. **Countertop removal**: Score caulk around backsplash and perimeter before prying. Granite or stone may require lifting equipment.
5. **Flooring**: If replacing tile, keep subfloor intact. Mark any loose or hollow spots — these are change orders if subfloor damage is found underneath.
6. **Lead/asbestos**: Texture on walls (pre-1980), floor tiles (9"×9" vinyl, pre-1980) — test before disturbing.

## COMMON HIDDEN CONDITIONS IN KITCHEN DEMO

Document with photos immediately. Each is a potential change order:

- **Subfloor damage** under dishwasher or sink: caused by slow leaks. Replace before cabinet installation.
- **Mold in cabinets under sink**: caused by unchecked leaks. Remediate before new cabinet install.
- **Out-of-plumb or out-of-level walls**: more than 1" over 8 feet requires shimming, scribing, or furring strips on cabinets. Takes extra time.
- **Electrical deficiencies**: no dedicated circuits, no GFCI, undersized wiring, aluminum wiring.
- **No vent for range hood**: if adding range hood requiring exterior duct, routing through cabinets and wall is extra scope.
- **Structural post or beam inside cabinet run**: affects layout, requires engineer review.

## PLUMBING ROUGH-IN

### Sink
- Drain stub: 18"–20" AFF, centered in cabinet or per vanity layout.
- Supply stubs: hot left, cold right at 18"–22" AFF, 8" apart horizontally (matches most faucet rough-in templates).
- Install 1/4-turn angle stops at both supply stubs before cabinet install.
- P-trap arm length: max 24" to vent (verify with inspector).

### Dishwasher
- Hot supply stub at 18" AFF (dedicated 1/4-turn stop preferred).
- Discharge: high-loop or air gap (check local code) to garbage disposal or drain above P-trap.
- Air gap required where mandated (California, some municipalities). Mount through countertop or sink deck.

### Garbage Disposal
- 120V dedicated circuit preferred (see electrical section).
- Drain: connects to disposal flange, then to P-trap.
- If disposal is new: verify sink drain hole fits disposal flange diameter.

### Ice Maker (Refrigerator)
- Cold supply stub: 1/4" OD copper or 1/4" compression fitting, shut-off valve behind refrigerator or in adjacent cabinet.
- Run before cabinets are installed if stub is inside cabinet run.

## ELECTRICAL ROUGH-IN

### NEC Requirements for Kitchens (NEC 210.52(C))
- **Minimum 2 small appliance circuits**: dedicated 20A circuits for countertop receptacles. No other outlets may be on these circuits (no refrigerator, disposal, dishwasher).
- **Countertop outlets**: every countertop section 12" or wider needs an outlet; no point on countertop more than 24" from an outlet. Outlets needed on islands ≥ 12"×24".
- **GFCI required**: all countertop receptacles (NEC 210.8). All outlets within 6 feet of a sink.
- **AFCI required**: all 15A and 20A circuits in kitchen living areas (NEC 210.12).

### Dedicated Circuits
- **Refrigerator**: dedicated 20A, 120V (NEC 210.52(B)(1)).
- **Dishwasher**: dedicated 20A, 120V.
- **Garbage disposal**: dedicated 20A, 120V (some codes allow shared with dishwasher — verify locally).
- **Microwave**: dedicated 20A, 120V if built-in or over-range.
- **Range/cooktop (electric)**: 240V, 40A or 50A depending on unit. #8 wire for 40A, #6 for 50A.
- **Range/cooktop (gas)**: 120V, 20A for ignition and oven controls.
- **Range hood**: 120V, 15–20A. If fan is large (600+ CFM), dedicated circuit.

### Outlet Heights
- Countertop outlets: 18"–20" AFF (above finished floor) standard; 4" above backsplash tile if backsplash is thick.
- Under-cabinet lighting: low-voltage transformer rough-in behind upper cabinet.
- Under-island outlets: flush pop-up or recessed outlets if countertop overhang exceeds 12".

## RANGE HOOD / VENTILATION

### Duct vs. Recirculating
- **Exterior ducted** (strongly preferred): removes grease, steam, and combustion byproducts.
- **Recirculating**: filters grease and odors but returns air to kitchen. Lower performance. Acceptable where exterior duct is impossible.

### Exterior Duct Requirements
- Minimum duct size: match hood manufacturer spec. Most residential hoods require 6" round or 3.25"×10" rectangular. Do NOT reduce duct size — increases static pressure, reduces CFM, and voids warranty.
- Route: shortest possible path to exterior. Each 90° elbow = 10 equivalent feet of straight duct.
- Backdraft damper: built into hood or at exterior termination — prevents cold air infiltration.
- Exterior termination: weatherproof cap, not a standard dryer vent cap. Keep away from windows/doors (min 3 feet).
- If routing through cabinets: line duct chase with drywall for fire protection if adjacent to combustibles.

### Makeup Air
- Hoods over 400 CFM in tight homes (post-2012 construction, sealed houses): makeup air system may be required by mechanical code.
- Without makeup air: large hoods can depressurize the house, pulling combustion gases from water heaters or furnaces into living space (backdraft).

## CABINET LAYOUT AND INSTALLATION

### Pre-Install Checks
- Walls plumb and flat: scribe rails and fillers are the fix for out-of-plumb, not forcing cabinets.
- Floor level: use a laser or long level to find high point of floor. Cabinets start from the high point, shimmed up from there.
- Locate all studs. Every cabinet must be screwed into at least 2 studs.

### Installation Sequence
1. Find high point of floor. Mark a level line at 34.5" (base cabinet height before countertop).
2. Mark second level line for top of upper cabinets (typically 84" or 96" AFF).
3. Hang upper cabinets first — harder to install after base cabinets are in place.
4. Use a ledger board at 54" AFF (standard) temporarily screwed to wall to support upper cabinets while screwing.
5. Screw cabinets to studs with 3" screws through the hanging rail. Minimum 2 screws per cabinet into studs.
6. Screw adjacent cabinets together through face frame (inside cabinet): 1.25" screws through pre-drilled holes.
7. Install base cabinets: shim level, screw to wall studs, screw to each other.
8. Filler pieces: scribe to wall, cut with jigsaw.

## COUNTERTOP READINESS

### Before Template (Stone/Engineered Stone)
- Sink cutout location confirmed with homeowner (mockup or template on paper).
- All cabinets level to within 1/8" across the run — countertop fabricator will require this.
- Sink must be on-site for undermount template cutout.
- Appliance locations confirmed (cooktop cutout must be exact).
- Lead time: stone countertops typically 2–3 weeks from template to install.

### Laminate (Field-Measure and Cut)
- Same levelness requirements.
- No backsplash to be installed until after countertop.

### Sink Installation
- Undermount: requires solid substrate. Install before countertop is lowered.
- Drop-in: install after countertop is in place.
- Farmhouse/apron: requires cabinet modification for apron front. Confirm before ordering cabinets.

## FLOORING TRANSITIONS

- **Install flooring after cabinets and before appliances**: this is the industry standard. Flooring goes under refrigerator and dishwasher, NOT under base cabinets.
- **If using tile**: grout lines must align with any adjacent rooms' tile (discuss with client before layout).
- **Toe kicks**: install after flooring, not before. Toe kick height adjusts for actual floor thickness.
- **Transition strips**: between kitchen and adjacent rooms at doorways. Confirm material and color selection with client before ordering.

## INSPECTION CHECKPOINTS

### Rough-In Inspection
- [ ] Plumbing: supply lines pressure-tested, drain installed with correct slope and vent.
- [ ] Electrical: all circuits installed, GFCI locations correct, AFCI breakers, dedicated circuits verified.
- [ ] Range hood duct: route, size, and termination cap in place.
- [ ] Blocking installed for any wall-mount elements.
- [ ] All hidden conditions (mold, rot) remediated and documented.
- [ ] Permit posted.

### Pre-Countertop Inspection (Cabinet QC)
- [ ] All cabinets level to within 1/8" across run.
- [ ] All cabinets plumb.
- [ ] All cabinets secured to studs (test by pushing firmly — no movement).
- [ ] All scribe pieces fitted.
- [ ] Cabinet interiors clean and damage-free.

### Final Inspection
- [ ] Sink operational, no leaks at any connection.
- [ ] Dishwasher cycles correctly, no leaks under.
- [ ] Disposal operational, air gap or high-loop correct.
- [ ] All outlets GFCI-protected and tested.
- [ ] Refrigerator in place, ice maker connected (if applicable).
- [ ] Range hood on, exhaust confirmed at exterior (tissue test).
- [ ] Flooring transitions complete.
- [ ] All trim and hardware installed.

## EVIDENCE REQUIRED FOR APPROVAL

- Photo: plumbing pressure test gauge (100 PSI, 15 min hold).
- Photo: range hood duct at exterior termination cap.
- Photo: GFCI outlet locations (countertop and sink).
- Photo: electrical panel showing new breakers labeled.
- Photo: cabinets level — laser level visible in photo spanning the run.
- Photo: all appliances installed and operational.
- Photo: permit and inspection card.

## COMMON CHANGE ORDER TRIGGERS

| Discovery | Typical Scope | Cost Range |
|---|---|---|
| Subfloor damage under dishwasher/sink | Replace subfloor | $400–$1,200 |
| No exterior duct path for range hood | Add duct through ceiling/wall | $300–$900 |
| Electrical upgrade needed (no dedicated circuits) | Run 2–4 new circuits | $600–$1,800 |
| Out-of-plumb walls > 1" | Fur out or scribe all cabinets | $200–$600 |
| Mold under cabinets | Remediation | $500–$2,500+ |
| Structural beam in cabinet run | Engineer review + rework | $800–$3,000+ |
| Makeup air required (>400 CFM hood) | Makeup air unit + duct | $800–$2,000 |
| No GFCI protection on existing countertop circuits | Replace all countertop outlets | $300–$600 |
