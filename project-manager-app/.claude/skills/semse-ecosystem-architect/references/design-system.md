# SEMSE Design and Experience Principles

## Experience objective

SEMSE should feel sophisticated without becoming cold, immersive without becoming distracting, and advanced without becoming obscure.

## Visual grammar

Use:

- scale;
- contrast;
- spacing;
- depth;
- composition;
- photography;
- product evidence;
- connected diagrams;
- transitions with meaning.

Do not use borders and cards as the primary method of organization.

## Section design

Every section needs:

- one dominant idea;
- one dominant visual behavior;
- a defined narrative function;
- a clear hierarchy;
- a reason to exist;
- an intentional mobile version.

## Rhythm

Alternate:

- light and dark moments;
- dense and quiet moments;
- human and system imagery;
- static and animated sequences;
- vision and evidence.

Avoid identical section structures repeated down the page.

## Images

Choose images that communicate:

- real people making decisions;
- teams coordinating;
- operators working;
- infrastructure;
- communities;
- sectors;
- real consequences of execution.

Avoid generic corporate scenes and AI clichés.

## Motion

Motion must orient, explain, connect, confirm, or reveal progress.

Respect reduced motion. Do not trap scrolling or delay access to essential content.

## Accessibility

Require:

- semantic HTML;
- keyboard navigation;
- visible focus;
- sufficient contrast;
- useful alternative text;
- touch-safe controls;
- meaningful error messages;
- non-color state indicators;
- reduced-motion support.

## Performance

Immersion does not justify slow delivery.

Use:

- responsive images;
- modern formats;
- lazy loading;
- video posters;
- progressive loading;
- restrained JavaScript;
- efficient transforms and opacity for motion.

## Scope note for this repository

Most work currently open in `project-manager-app` (see `docs/AUDIT_REMEDIATION_PLAN.md`) is data/security/permissions correctness inside the existing authenticated app, not new visual design. Apply this document when a task is actually about narrative, layout, or visual direction — do not force a design pass onto a fix that is really a data or RBAC bug.
